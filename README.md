      <h2>The 14-Lesson Playbook</h2>

      <div class="lesson">
        <div class="lesson-number">1</div>
        <div>
          <h3>What TikTok Shop Affiliate Is</h3>
          <p>
            TikTok Shop Affiliate lets creators promote eligible products from sellers and earn commission when viewers purchase through their shoppable content. The creator does not have to package, ship, or fulfill the product, but they do need to create content that builds trust and helps the viewer make a buying decision.
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Creators recommend products through videos, LIVE streams, and product links.</li>
            <li>Sellers use affiliate collaborations to get creator content and performance-based sales.</li>
            <li>Commission is normally connected to tracked product sales, not just views.</li>
            <li>The creator's job is to match the right product with the right audience and explain the value clearly.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>
      <article class="lesson">
        <h3>Lesson 1: Understanding the TikTok Shop Ecosystem</h3>
        <table>
          <thead><tr><th>Role</th><th>What They Do</th><th>Revenue Source</th></tr></thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr><td>Seller</td><td>Lists products, manages fulfillment, handles customers.</td><td>Product sales.</td></tr>
            <tr><td>Affiliate Creator</td><td>Promotes seller products through videos, LIVE sessions, and showcase links.</td><td>Commission per sale.</td></tr>
            <tr><td>TikTok</td><td>Provides shopping infrastructure, checkout, discovery, and policy enforcement.</td><td>Platform and transaction fees.</td></tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
        <p>As an affiliate creator, your job is not to carry inventory. Your job is to make product content that gets attention, earns trust, and moves people to purchase.</p>
      </article>

      <div class="lesson">
        <div class="lesson-number">2</div>
        <div>
          <h3>Account Setup and Eligibility Check</h3>
          <p>
            Before creating content, students should confirm whether their account can access TikTok Shop affiliate features. The exact path can vary, but the goal is to find the creator monetization or shop area inside TikTok, review requirements, complete any identity or tax steps, and understand where product links and performance data will live.
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Check TikTok app, Creator Center, and TikTok Shop tools for current affiliate access.</li>
            <li>Confirm age, region, account standing, and any follower or content requirements shown by TikTok.</li>
            <li>Complete profile basics: clear bio, niche signal, profile photo, and contact path if needed.</li>
            <li>Review payout setup, tax forms, and account verification before expecting commissions.</li>
          </ul>
          <div class="callout warning">
            <strong>Do not skip this:</strong> If an account is not eligible yet, students can still prepare by improving their niche, posting product-style content, and building trust before applying again.
      <article class="lesson">
        <h3>Lesson 2: Getting Approved</h3>
        <div class="grid">
          <div class="card">
            <h3>Common Requirements</h3>
            <ul>
              <li>Creator must be 18 or older.</li>
              <li>Account must be active and in good standing.</li>
              <li>Creator must be in a supported TikTok Shop market.</li>
              <li>Follower thresholds may apply depending on region and program access.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div class="card">
            <h3>If You Are Not Approved Yet</h3>
            <ul>
              <li>Post niche content daily until your account qualifies.</li>
              <li>Apply for targeted collaborations from individual sellers.</li>
              <li>Build a clean profile around one product category.</li>
              <li>Keep violations, repost spam, and low-quality engagement off the account.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>
      </article>

      <article class="lesson">
        <h3>Lesson 3: Finding Winning Products</h3>
        <p>Product selection is half the game. Look inside TikTok Shop Affiliate Center, the TikTok Shop tab, trending hashtags, and TikTok Creative Center. Favor products with proven sales, strong reviews, fast shipping, clear demonstrations, and a commission that makes the effort worth it.</p>
        <table>
          <thead><tr><th>Criteria</th><th>What to Look For</th></tr></thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr><td>Commission</td><td>10%+ preferred, but lower can work if volume and conversion are strong.</td></tr>
            <tr><td>Price Point</td><td>$20-$80 is often impulse-friendly while still producing meaningful commission.</td></tr>
            <tr><td>Proof</td><td>Recent reviews, sales history, creator content, and comment demand.</td></tr>
            <tr><td>Demo Value</td><td>Before/after, satisfying use, visible transformation, or strong problem solving.</td></tr>
            <tr><td>Shipping</td><td>Fast domestic shipping whenever possible.</td></tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </article>

      <div class="lesson">
        <div class="lesson-number">3</div>
        <div>
          <h3>Choosing the Right Product Category</h3>
          <p>
            Beginners often pick products only because the commission looks high. That is backwards. A product should fit the creator's audience, be easy to demonstrate, solve a clear problem, and have enough proof to make a viewer feel safe buying.
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Audience fit: the product should make sense for the people already watching.</li>
            <li>Visual proof: the result should be easy to show on camera.</li>
            <li>Problem clarity: the product should solve a pain point people recognize quickly.</li>
            <li>Purchase simplicity: the viewer should understand what it is and why they need it in seconds.</li>
            <li>Seller reliability: look for product reviews, shop activity, shipping expectations, and customer feedback.</li>
          </ul>
      <article class="lesson">
        <h3>Lesson 4: Setting Up Your Showcase</h3>
        <ol>
          <li>Open Product Marketplace in TikTok Shop Affiliate Center.</li>
          <li>Add relevant products to your showcase.</li>
          <li>Keep the showcase tight: 10-30 products in one clear niche.</li>
          <li>Put your highest-converting products near the top.</li>
          <li>Remove products that get clicks but no orders after enough testing.</li>
        </ol>
        <div class="callout">
          Your showcase is a storefront. Treat it like one. Random products make the account feel messy and confuse TikTok about who should see your content.
        </div>
      </div>
      </article>

      <div class="lesson">
